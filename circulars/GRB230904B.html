GCN Circular 34615: GRB 230904B: BALROG localization (Fermi Trigger 715526984 / GRB 230904562)

Event ID: GRB 230904B
Submitter: Jochen Greiner at MPE <jcgrog@mpe.mpg.de>
Created: 2023-09-04 14:01:47 UTC
Source file: 34615.json

T. Preis, B. Biltzinger, J. Burgess & J. Greiner (all MPE Garching) report:

The public trigdat data of the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) trigger
715526984 at 13:29:39 on 04 Sept. 2023 were automatically fitted for spectrum
and sky location with BALROG (Burgess et al. 2018, MNRAS 476, 1427;
Berlato et al. 2019, ApJ 873, 60).

The best-fit position (1 sigma statistical errors) is:
RA(2000.0) = 20.8+/-2.4 deg
Decl.(2000.0) = -26.7+/-0.9 deg
We estimate an additional systematic error of 2 deg.

Further details are available at:
https://grb.mpe.mpg.de/grb/GRB230904562/

The Healpix map can be downloaded from:
https://grb.mpe.mpg.de/grb/GRB230904562/healpix

The location parameters are available as JSON at:
https://grb.mpe.mpg.de/grb/GRB230904562/json

GCN Circular 34617: GRB 230904B is not a GRB

Event ID: GRB 230904B
Submitter: Oliver J Roberts at USRA/NASA <oliver.roberts@nasa.gov>
Created: 2023-09-04 16:39:00 UTC
Source file: 34617.json

Subject: GRB 230904B is not a GRB

O.J. Roberts (USRA) and S. Bala (USRA), report on behalf of
the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor Team:

"The Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) trigger 715526984/230904562 at 13:29:39.52 UT
on 04 September 2023, previously named as GRB 230904B (GCN 34615), is in fact not due
to a GRB. This trigger is due to particle activity."
