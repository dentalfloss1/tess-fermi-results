GCN Circular 43765: GRB 260216A: Fermi GBM Final Real-time Localization

Event ID: GRB 260216A
Submitter: Fermi GBM Team at MSFC/Fermi-GBM <do_not_reply@GIOC.nsstc.nasa.gov>
Created: 2026-02-16 21:26:38 UTC

The Fermi GBM team reports the detection of a likely LONG GRB

At 21:15:57 UT on 16 Feb 2026, the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) triggered and located GRB 260216A (trigger 792969362.07265 / 260216886).

The on-ground calculated location, using the Fermi GBM trigger data, is RA = 292.5, Dec = -25.0 (J2000 degrees, equivalent to J2000 19h 30m, -25d 00'), with a statistical uncertainty of 12.6 degrees.

The angle from the Fermi LAT boresight is 127.0 degrees.

GCN Circular 43766: GRB 260216A: Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor trigger 792969362/260216886 is not a GRB

Event ID: GRB 260216A
Submitter: Boyan A. Hristov at UAH <boyan.a.hristov@nasa.gov>
Created: 2026-02-17 04:23:20 UTC
Source file: 43766.json

B. Hristov (UAH) reports on behalf of the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor Team:

"The Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) trigger 792969362/260216886 at 21:15:57.07 UT
on 16 February 2026, tentatively classified as a GRB, is in fact not due
to a GRB. This trigger is likely due to distant particles."
