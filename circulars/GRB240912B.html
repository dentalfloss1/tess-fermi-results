GCN Circular 37472: GRB 240912B: Fermi GBM Final Real-time Localization

The Fermi GBM team reports the detection of a likely LONG GRB

At 12:38:00 UT on 12 Sep 2024, the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) triggered and located GRB 240912B (trigger 747837485.316546 / 240912526).

The on-ground calculated location, using the Fermi GBM trigger data, is RA = 226.1, Dec = 42.6 (J2000 degrees, equivalent to J2000 15h 04m, 42d 36'), with a statistical uncertainty of 2.0 degrees.

The angle from the Fermi LAT boresight is 147.0 degrees.

GCN Circular 37488: Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor trigger 747837485/240912526 is not a GRB

Event ID:
Submitter: Elisabetta Bissaldi at Politecnico and INFN Bari <elisabetta.bissaldi@ba.infn.it>
Created: 2024-09-13 14:32:56 UTC
Source file: 37488.json

E. Bissaldi (Politecnico and INFN Bari) reports on behalf of the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor Team:

"The Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) trigger 747837485/240912526 at 12:38:00.32 UT
on 12 September 2024, tentatively classified as GRB 240912B (GCN #37472), is in fact not due
to a GRB. This trigger is likely due to local particles."
