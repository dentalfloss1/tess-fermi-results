The short-duration GRB 221022C
(Fermi-GBM detection: Poolakkil and Meegan, GCN Circ. 32836)
was detected by Fermi (GBM trigger 688142511), INTEGRAL (SPI-ACS),
Konus-Wind, and Swift (BAT) at about 52906 s UT (14:41:46).
The burst was outside the coded field of view of the BAT.

We have triangulated it to two preliminary, 3 sigma error boxes
whose coordinates are:

dimension is 6.91 deg (the minimum one is 12.4 arcmin).
The Sun distance was 50 deg.

This localization may be improved.

The IPN localization is consistent with, but reduces the area of,
the Fermi-GBM final localization (glg_healpix_all_bn221022612_v00).

GCN Circular 32901: Konus-Wind detection of GRB 221022C

Event ID: GRB 221022C
Submitter: Anna Ridnaia at Ioffe Institute <ridnaia@mail.ioffe.ru>
Created: 2022-10-31 14:54:50 UTC
Source file: 32901.json

A.Ridnaia, D. Frederiks, A.Lysenko, D. Svinkin,
A. Tsvetkova,  M. Ulanov, and T. Cline,
on behalf of the Konus-Wind team, report:

The short-duration GRB 221022C
(IPN triangulation: Svinkin et al., GCN Circ. 32857)
triggered Konus-Wind at T0=52903.608 s UT (14:41:43.608).

The burst light curve shows a multipeaked structure,
which starts at T0-88 ms and has a total duration of ~128 ms.

The Konus-Wind light curve of this GRB is available at
http://www.ioffe.ru/LEA/GRBs/GRB221022_T52903/

As observed by Konus-Wind, the burst
had a fluence of 8.55(-1.23,+1.68)x10^-7 erg/cm2,
and a 16-ms peak flux, measured from T0-0.012 s,
of 2.01(-0.39,+0.47)x10^-5 erg/cm2/s
(both in the 20 keV - 10 MeV energy range).

Since the main fraction of the burst emission was detected
before the trigger, the spectral analysis was performed using
the KW 3-channel light curve data.

Modelling the KW 3-channel time-integrated spectrum
(measured from T0-0.088 s to T0+0.040 s)
by a power law with exponential cutoff model:
dN/dE ~ (E^alpha)*exp(-E*(2+alpha)/Ep),
yields alpha = -0.09(-0.28,+0.40) and Ep = 548(-86,+121) keV.

All the quoted errors are at the 68% confidence level.
All the quoted values are preliminary.
